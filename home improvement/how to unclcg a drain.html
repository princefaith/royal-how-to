How to Unclog a Drain: Simple Methods That Work

A clogged drain is a common household problem that can often be fixed without calling a plumber. This guide will show you several easy methods to clear a clogged drain using simple tools and ingredients you probably already have at home.

What You'll Need

Depending on the method you choose, you might need:

  • Plunger
  • Baking soda
  • White vinegar
  • Hot water
  • Salt
  • Drain snake or wire hanger
  • Bucket
  • Rubber gloves
Tools Image

Method 1: The Plunger

Start with a plunger, as it's often the most effective tool for drain clogs.

Cover the overflow opening with a wet cloth if you're working on a sink. Place the plunger over the drain and push down firmly, then pull up quickly. Repeat this pumping action several times until the water begins to drain.

Plunger Image

Method 2: Baking Soda and Vinegar

This natural method works well for mild clogs and helps clean your pipes.

Pour about half a cup of baking soda down the drain. Follow with half a cup of white vinegar. Cover the drain to contain the fizzing reaction. Let it sit for 30 minutes, then flush with hot water.

Baking Soda Image

Method 3: Hot Water and Salt

For grease-related clogs, try this simple method.

Mix half a cup of salt with half a cup of baking soda and pour it down the drain. Let it sit for 30 minutes, then slowly pour a pot of boiling water down the drain. Repeat if necessary.

Salt Image

Method 4: The Drain Snake

For stubborn clogs, a drain snake can reach deep into the pipes.

Insert the snake into the drain and push it through until you feel resistance. Twist and push to break up the clog. Pull out the snake and clean off any debris. Flush the drain with hot water.

Snake Image

Preventing Future Clogs

Once you've cleared the clog, take steps to prevent future problems.

Use drain strainers to catch hair and food particles. Avoid pouring grease down the drain. Once a month, flush drains with hot water and baking soda to keep them clear.

Prevention Image

Important Tips

  • Never mix chemical drain cleaners with other products
  • For severe clogs, you may need to repeat methods multiple times
  • Be patient - some methods take time to work
  • If multiple drains are clogged, you may have a main line issue
  • When in doubt, call a professional plumber
