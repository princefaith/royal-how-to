How to Fix a Flat Tire: A Simple Guide

A flat tire can happen to anyone, but you don't need to call for help if you know how to fix it yourself. This guide will show you the simple steps to change a flat tire and get back on the road safely.

What You'll Need

Before you start, make sure you have these items:

  • Spare tire (check it's properly inflated)
  • Car jack
  • Lug wrench
  • Wheel wedges
  • Flashlight (if it's dark)
  • Gloves (to keep your hands clean)
  • Reflective triangles or flares for safety
Tools Image

Step 1: Find a Safe Location

If you get a flat while driving, slow down and pull over to a safe, flat area away from traffic.

Turn on your hazard lights and apply the parking brake. Place wheel wedges behind the wheels to prevent the car from rolling.

Step 1 Image

Step 2: Remove the Hubcap and Loosen Lug Nuts

Use the flat end of your lug wrench to remove the hubcap if your car has one.

Then, use the wrench to loosen the lug nuts by turning them counterclockwise. Don't remove them completely yet - just break their resistance.

Step 2 Image

Step 3: Jack Up the Vehicle

Place the jack under the metal frame of your vehicle near the flat tire. Most cars have a designated jack point - check your owner's manual.

Raise the vehicle until the flat tire is about 6 inches off the ground. Never put any part of your body under the vehicle when it's supported only by a jack.

Step 3 Image

Step 4: Remove the Flat Tire

Now fully unscrew the lug nuts and remove them. Place them in a safe spot where they won't roll away.

Grip the tire by the treads and pull it straight toward you until it's free from the wheel base. Set it flat on the ground so it doesn't roll away.

Step 4 Image

Step 5: Mount the Spare Tire

Lift the spare tire and line up the holes with the lug bolts. Push the spare onto the wheel base until it can't go any further.

Put the lug nuts back on and tighten them by hand as much as you can. Don't use the wrench yet.

Step 5 Image

Step 6: Lower the Vehicle and Tighten Lug Nuts

Carefully lower the vehicle with the jack until the spare tire touches the ground but isn't supporting the full weight of the car.

Use the wrench to tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to ensure even tightening. Then fully lower the vehicle and remove the jack.

Step 6 Image

Step 7: Check the Pressure and Clean Up

Check the pressure in your spare tire. If it's low, drive carefully to the nearest gas station to fill it.

Put your flat tire, tools, and jack back in your trunk. Don't forget to remove your wheel wedges and reflective triangles.

Step 7 Image

Important Safety Tips

  • Always change a tire on flat, solid ground away from traffic
  • Never change a tire on a slope or soft ground
  • Use your parking brake and wheel wedges for safety
  • Don't rely on a spare tire for long distances or high speeds
  • Get your flat tire repaired or replaced as soon as possible
  • Practice changing a tire at home before you need to do it on the road
