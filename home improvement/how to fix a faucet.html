How to Fix a Leaky Faucet: A Simple Guide

A dripping faucet is annoying and wastes water. Many faucet problems are easy to fix yourself with basic tools. This guide will show you how to fix the most common faucet issues step by step.

What You'll Need

Gather these tools before you start:

  • Adjustable wrench
  • Screwdrivers (flathead and Phillips)
  • Replacement washers or O-rings
  • Pliers
  • Allen wrench set
  • Towels or rags
  • Bucket or container
  • Plumber's grease
Tools Image

Step 1: Turn Off the Water

Find the water shut-off valves under the sink. Turn them clockwise to shut off the water.

If there are no valves under the sink, you may need to turn off the main water supply to your house.

Turn on the faucet to release any remaining water pressure and drain the lines.

Step 1 Image

Step 2: Identify Your Faucet Type

There are several types of faucets:

  • Compression faucets (have separate hot and cold handles)
  • Ball faucets (single handle that moves around)
  • Cartridge faucets (single handle that moves up and down)
  • Ceramic disk faucets (single handle with smooth operation)

Knowing your faucet type helps you get the right replacement parts.

Step 2 Image

Step 3: Take Apart the Faucet

Remove the handle. Look for a small screw under a decorative cap. Use a screwdriver to remove it.

Lift off the handle to expose the inner parts. Place all parts in order on a towel so you remember how to put them back.

Use your wrench to loosen and remove the packing nut.

Step 3 Image

Step 4: Replace the Worn Parts

Remove the stem assembly from the faucet body.

Check the washers and O-rings for wear or damage. These are usually the cause of leaks.

Take the old parts to a hardware store to find exact matches. Replace all worn parts.

Apply plumber's grease to new rubber parts before installing them.

Step 4 Image

Step 5: Reassemble the Faucet

Put the faucet back together in reverse order of how you took it apart.

Make sure all parts are properly seated and aligned.

Tighten the packing nut snugly but don't overtighten it.

Reattach the handle and replace any decorative caps.

Step 5 Image

Step 6: Test for Leaks

Slowly turn the water supply back on.

Check for leaks around the handles and base of the faucet.

Turn on the faucet and let the water run for a minute to clear any debris.

If it still leaks, you may need to tighten connections or replace additional parts.

Step 6 Image

Helpful Tips

  • Take photos as you disassemble the faucet to help with reassembly
  • Place a towel in the sink drain to catch small parts
  • Replace all washers and O-rings even if they look okay
  • If your faucet has ceramic disks, they rarely need replacement
  • For stubborn parts, use penetrating oil before forcing them
  • If you're unsure about your faucet type, take photos to show at the hardware store
