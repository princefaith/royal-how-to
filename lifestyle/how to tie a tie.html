How to Tie a Tie: The Simple Way

Learning to tie a tie is a useful skill for many occasions. This guide will show you the simple way to tie a tie that works for most situations. With a little practice, you'll be able to do it without thinking.

What You'll Need

Before you start, make sure you have:

  • A necktie
  • A mirror to check your work
  • About 5 minutes of time
Tie Image

Step 1: Starting Position

put the tie around your neck with the wide end on your right side and the narrow end on your left. The wide end should hang about 12 inches lower than the narrow end.

Adjust the tie so the seam is facing inward, not showing outward.

Step 1 Image

Step 2: Cross the Wide End

Take the wide end and cross it over the narrow end. Hold this cross point with your left hand.

Make sure the wide end is completely over the narrow end, not under it.

Step 2 Image

Step 3: Bring Up and Through

Bring the wide end up through the loop around your neck. Pull it down toward your chest.

Keep holding the cross point with your left hand to keep it secure.

Step 3 Image

Step 4: Across and Through Again

Take the wide end and bring it across the front from right to left.

Then bring it up through the neck loop again and pull it down through the knot in front.

Step 4 Image

Step 5: Tighten and Adjust

Hold the narrow end and slide the knot up toward your collar.

Gently tighten the knot by pulling down on the wide end. Adjust until it feels comfortable and looks neat.

Step 5 Image

Helpful Tips

  • Practice a few times when you're not in a hurry
  • The tip of your tie should reach your belt buckle when tied correctly
  • If the tie is too long or short, start over with the wide end higher or lower
  • For a dimple just below the knot, create a slight crease as you tighten
