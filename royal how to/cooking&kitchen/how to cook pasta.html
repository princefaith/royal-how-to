How to Cook Pasta: A Simple Guide

Cooking pasta is one of the basic kitchen skills everyone should know. This guide will show you how to cook perfect pasta every time - not too hard, not too soft, just right.

What You'll Need

Gather these items before you start:

  • Pasta of your choice
  • Water
  • Salt
  • A large pot
  • A colander or strainer
  • A timer or clock
Pasta Ingredients Image

Step 1: Boil the Water

Fill a large pot about two-thirds full with water. Place it on the stove and turn the heat to high.

Put the lid on to help the water boil faster. Wait until you see big bubbles rising to the surface.

Boiling Water Image

Step 2: Add Salt

Once the water is boiling, add about 1 tablespoon of salt for every 4 cups of water.

The salt makes the pasta taste better. Don't add oil - it prevents sauce from sticking to the pasta.

Adding Salt Image

Step 3: Add the Pasta

Slowly add the pasta to the boiling water. Stir immediately with a spoon to prevent sticking.

Keep the heat high so the water continues to boil. You may need to adjust the temperature slightly.

Adding Pasta Image

Step 4: Cook and Stir

Check the package for cooking time. Set a timer for 1-2 minutes less than recommended.

Stir the pasta occasionally while it cooks. Taste a piece when the timer goes off to see if it's done.

Stirring Pasta Image

Step 5: Drain the Pasta

When the pasta is cooked to your liking, turn off the heat.

Carefully pour the pasta and water into a colander in the sink. Shake gently to remove excess water.

Draining Pasta Image

Step 6: Serve Immediately

Transfer the pasta to a bowl or plate. Add your favorite sauce right away while it's still hot.

If you're not using sauce immediately, toss with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.

Served Pasta Image

Helpful Tips

  • Use plenty of water - pasta needs room to move while cooking
  • Save a cup of pasta water before draining - it's great for thinning sauces
  • Test pasta a minute before the recommended time - it continues cooking after draining
  • Different shapes of pasta have different cooking times
  • Fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried pasta
  • Don't rinse pasta after draining unless making a cold pasta salad
