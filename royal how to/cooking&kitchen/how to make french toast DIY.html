How to Make French Toast: A Simple Breakfast

French toast is a delicious breakfast that's easy to make with just a few basic ingredients. This guide will show you how to make perfect French toast every time, with a crispy outside and soft inside.

What You'll Need

Gather these ingredients before you start:

  • 4 slices of bread (slightly stale works best)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • Butter or oil for cooking
  • Maple syrup or powdered sugar for serving
Ingredients Image

Step 1: Make the Egg Mixture

In a shallow bowl or plate with edges, crack the eggs and whisk them until the yolks and whites are mixed together.

Add the milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt. Whisk everything together until it's well combined and smooth.

Step 1 Image

Step 2: Soak the Bread

Place a slice of bread into the egg mixture. Let it soak for about 20-30 seconds on each side.

Don't soak it too long or the bread will become mushy and hard to handle. You want it to absorb the liquid but still hold its shape.

Step 2 Image

Step 3: Cook the French Toast

Heat a frying pan or griddle over medium heat. Add a little butter or oil to coat the surface.

Place the soaked bread in the pan. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown and slightly crispy.

Step 3 Image

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Remove the French toast from the pan and place it on a plate.

Serve immediately with maple syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruit, or any toppings you like.

Step 4 Image

Helpful Tips

  • Use slightly stale bread for better absorption without falling apart
  • Add a pinch of nutmeg to the egg mixture for extra flavor
  • Keep cooked French toast warm in the oven at low temperature while making the rest
  • Try different types of bread like brioche, challah, or French bread
  • For a richer taste, use half-and-half instead of milk
