How to Fix a Toilet: Simple Repairs Anyone Can Do

A running or leaking toilet can waste water and increase your bills. Many common toilet problems are easy to fix yourself with basic tools. This guide will show you how to handle the most common toilet repairs.

What You'll Need

Before you start, gather these tools and supplies:

  • Plunger
  • Adjustable wrench
  • Screwdrivers (flathead and Phillips)
  • New flapper or fill valve (if needed)
  • Towels or rags for cleanup
  • Bucket
  • Rubber gloves
Tools Image

Step 1: Identify the Problem

First, figure out what's wrong with your toilet:

  • Is the toilet running constantly?
  • Is there water on the floor around the base?
  • Does the toilet not flush properly?
  • Is the tank not filling correctly?

Listen for running water and look for visible leaks.

Step 1 Image

Step 2: Fix a Running Toilet

Remove the tank lid and check the flapper. The flapper is the rubber seal at the bottom of the tank.

If water is leaking past the flapper, it may need to be cleaned or replaced. Turn off the water supply, flush to empty the tank, and replace the flapper if it's worn or damaged.

Step 2 Image

Step 3: Adjust the Fill Valve

If the toilet keeps running, the fill valve might need adjustment.

The fill valve controls how much water enters the tank. Look for an adjustment screw or clip on the valve. Lower the float to make the tank fill with less water.

Step 3 Image

Step 4: Fix a Leaking Base

If water is on the floor around the toilet base, the wax seal might need replacement.

Turn off the water supply, flush to empty the tank and bowl. Remove the nuts at the base, lift the toilet, scrape off the old wax ring, and install a new one. Carefully reset the toilet and tighten the nuts.

Step 4 Image

Step 5: Unclog the Toilet

For a clogged toilet, use a plunger first.

Make sure there's enough water in the bowl to cover the plunger. Push down gently at first to remove air, then plunge vigorously up and down. Repeat several times if needed.

Step 5 Image

Important Safety Tips

  • Always turn off the water supply before working on your toilet
  • Have a bucket ready to catch any remaining water
  • Don't overtighten connections as porcelain can crack easily
  • If you're unsure about a repair, call a professional plumber
  • Keep a plunger handy for quick clogs
