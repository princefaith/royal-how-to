How to Lay a Tile Backsplash: A DIY Guide

A tile backsplash can transform your kitchen or bathroom. It protects your walls from spills and splashes while adding style to your space. This guide will show you how to install a tile backsplash yourself, even if you've never done it before.

What You'll Need

Gather these materials and tools before starting:

  • Tile of your choice
  • Tile adhesive (mastic)
  • Grout
  • Tile spacers
  • Notched trowel
  • Tile cutter or wet saw
  • Grout float
  • Sponge and bucket
  • Measuring tape
  • Level
  • Safety glasses
Tools Image

Step 1: Prepare the Wall

Start with a clean, dry wall surface. Remove any old backsplash or loose material.

Wipe down the wall to remove grease or dirt. If you have painted walls, lightly sand them to help the adhesive stick better.

Use a level to draw a horizontal line where the bottom of your backsplash will be.

Step 1 Image

Step 2: Plan Your Layout

Lay out your tiles on a flat surface to plan the pattern. This helps you see how they'll look and where you might need to make cuts.

Measure the area and find the center point. It's usually best to start tiling from the center and work outward to both sides.

If you're using patterned tiles, arrange them in the order you plan to install them.

Step 2 Image

Step 3: Apply the Adhesive

Mix the adhesive according to the package directions. Using your notched trowel, spread a thin layer of adhesive on a small section of the wall.

Hold the trowel at a 45-degree angle to create ridges in the adhesive. These ridges help the tiles stick better.

Only spread as much adhesive as you can tile in about 15-20 minutes before it starts to dry.

Step 3 Image

Step 4: Place the Tiles

Press the first tile into the adhesive with a slight twisting motion. This helps it stick better.

Place spacers between tiles to keep even gaps for grout. Continue placing tiles, checking occasionally with your level to make sure they're straight.

For edges and corners, measure and cut tiles to fit using your tile cutter. Wear safety glasses when cutting tiles.

Step 4 Image

Step 5: Apply the Grout

Let the adhesive dry completely (usually 24 hours). Then remove the spacers.

Mix the grout according to package directions. Using a grout float, spread the grout over the tiles at a 45-degree angle, pushing it into the gaps.

Remove excess grout with the edge of the float. Let the grout set for about 15-20 minutes.

Step 5 Image

Step 6: Clean and Finish

Use a damp sponge to wipe away excess grout from the tile surfaces. Rinse your sponge frequently in clean water.

Make several passes with the sponge until the tiles are clean and the grout lines are smooth.

Let the grout dry for 24 hours, then apply a grout sealer if recommended for your type of grout.

Step 6 Image

Helpful Tips for Success

  • Buy 10% more tile than you need to account for cuts and mistakes
  • Work in small sections to prevent the adhesive from drying out
  • Use painter's tape to protect countertops and cabinets
  • For a professional look, make sure all cut edges face toward corners or under cabinets
  • If using natural stone tiles, seal them before grouting to prevent staining
  • Take your time with measuring and cutting - accuracy matters
