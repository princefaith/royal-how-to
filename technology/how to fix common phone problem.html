How to Fix Common Phone Problems Yourself

Phones can have problems sometimes, but you might not need to buy a new one or pay for expensive repairs. This guide shows you simple ways to fix common phone issues yourself.

When Your Phone Won't Turn On

If your phone doesn't turn on, try these steps before worrying:

  1. Plug your phone into a charger for at least 30 minutes
  2. Try a different charger and cable if you have one
  3. Check the power outlet by plugging in something else
  4. Press and hold the power button for 10-15 seconds

Sometimes the battery is completely dead and needs time to charge before it will turn on.

Fixing a Frozen Phone

If your phone is frozen and not responding:

  1. Press and hold the power button for 10-15 seconds
  2. If that doesn't work, press and hold both the power and volume down buttons together for 10 seconds
  3. Wait for the phone to restart

This is like restarting a computer when it freezes. It usually fixes the problem without losing any of your information.

Restart Image

When Apps Keep Crashing

If apps on your phone keep closing or not working:

  1. Close the app completely and open it again
  2. Restart your phone
  3. Check for updates to the app in your app store
  4. Check for updates to your phone's software
  5. If nothing works, delete the app and install it again
Remember

If you delete an app, you might lose information stored only in that app. Some apps save your data online, but others might not.

Fixing Phone Overheating

If your phone gets too hot:

  1. Stop using it and let it cool down
  2. Take it out of its case if it has one
  3. Move it out of direct sunlight
  4. Close apps you're not using
  5. Turn off features like Bluetooth and WiFi if you're not using them

Phones can get hot when they're working hard or when they're in a hot place. If your phone gets hot often, there might be a problem with the battery.

Solving Battery Drain Problems

If your battery doesn't last as long as it used to:

  1. Check which apps use the most battery in your settings
  2. Reduce screen brightness
  3. Turn off location services for apps that don't need it
  4. Close apps when you're done using them
  5. Turn on battery saver mode when your battery is low

All phone batteries wear out over time. After 2-3 years, you might need to replace the battery if it doesn't hold charge well.

Battery Image

Important Tips

  • Always back up your important information regularly
  • Keep your phone software updated
  • Don't install apps from unknown sources
  • If your phone gets wet, turn it off immediately and don't charge it until it's completely dry
  • If you're not sure about a repair, ask someone who knows more about phones
