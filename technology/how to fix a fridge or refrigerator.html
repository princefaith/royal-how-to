How to Fix Common Fridge Problems

Is your fridge not cooling properly? Making strange noises? Many common fridge problems can be fixed at home without calling a repair person. This guide will show you how to troubleshoot and fix the most common refrigerator issues.

Safety First

Always unplug your refrigerator before attempting any repairs. Be careful with electrical components and moving parts.

What You'll Need

Basic tools for most fridge repairs:

  • Screwdrivers (both flathead and Phillips)
  • Multimeter for testing electrical parts
  • Vacuum cleaner with brush attachment
  • Cleaning supplies
  • New water filter (if your fridge has a water dispenser)
Tools Image

Problem 1: Fridge Not Cooling Enough

If your fridge isn't keeping food cold enough, try these steps:

  1. Check the temperature setting - make sure it didn't get changed by accident
  2. Clean the condenser coils - dirty coils make the fridge work harder
  3. Make sure the door seals are clean and tight - close the door on a dollar bill; if it slips out easily, the seal may need replacing
  4. Check if the fridge is too full - air needs to circulate inside
Condenser Coils Image

Problem 2: Fridge Making Noise

Strange noises coming from your fridge? Here's what to check:

  1. Buzzing sound - often means the condenser fan is hitting something or needs cleaning
  2. Clicking noise - could be the start relay failing
  3. Gurgling or sizzling - usually normal sounds of refrigerant moving
  4. Knocking or rattling - check if the fridge is level; adjust the feet if needed
Fridge Fan Image

Problem 3: Water Leaking

Water under or inside your fridge? Try these fixes:

  1. Check the defrost drain - if clogged, water can overflow
  2. Look at the water supply line - check for leaks if your fridge has a water dispenser
  3. Inspect the door gasket - if damaged, moisture can get in and cause condensation
  4. Check the drain pan - if cracked, it will need replacement
Defrost Drain Image

Problem 4: Ice Maker Not Working

If your ice maker has stopped making ice:

  1. Check the water supply - make sure the valve is open
  2. Replace the water filter - a clogged filter can reduce water flow
  3. Check the ice maker arm - make sure it's in the down position
  4. Test the water inlet valve - use a multimeter to check if it's getting power
Ice Maker Image

Maintenance Tips to Prevent Problems

  • Clean condenser coils every 6 months
  • Check door seals regularly for cracks or wear
  • Keep the fridge filled but not overcrowded
  • Defrost manual-defrost freezers before ice buildup becomes too thick
  • Replace water filters every 6 months
