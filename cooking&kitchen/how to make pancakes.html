How to Make Perfect Pancakes

Making pancakes from scratch is easier than you think. This simple recipe will help you create fluffy, golden pancakes that taste much better than the boxed mix kind. Perfect for weekend breakfasts or a special treat.

What You'll Need

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter or oil
  • Butter or oil for cooking
  • Maple syrup for serving

Step 1: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Make sure everything is well combined.

This helps the baking powder distribute evenly so your pancakes will rise properly.

Dry Ingredients

Step 2: Mix the Wet Ingredients

In another bowl, beat the egg lightly. Then add the milk and melted butter or oil. Whisk until everything is well combined.

If you're using butter, make sure it's cooled slightly so it doesn't cook the egg when you mix it.

Wet Ingredients

Step 3: Combine Everything

Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir gently until just combined. It's okay if there are a few lumps.

Don't overmix! Overmixing makes pancakes tough instead of fluffy.

Combining

Step 4: Heat the Pan

Place a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Let it get hot before you add batter.

To test if it's ready, flick a few drops of water onto the surface. If they sizzle and dance, it's hot enough.

Heating Pan

Step 5: Cook the Pancakes

Lightly grease the pan with butter or oil. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake.

Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2-3 minutes. Then flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes until golden brown.

Cooking

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Serve the pancakes warm with maple syrup, butter, fresh fruit, or your favorite toppings.

If you're making a large batch, keep finished pancakes warm in a 200Â°F oven while you cook the rest.

Serving

Helpful Tips for Perfect Pancakes

  • Let the batter rest for 5-10 minutes before cooking for fluffier pancakes
  • Don't press down on the pancakes while cooking - this makes them dense
  • If you want blueberry pancakes, add fresh berries to the batter after pouring onto the griddle
  • For buttermilk pancakes, substitute buttermilk for regular milk and reduce baking powder to 1 teaspoon
  • If the pancakes are browning too quickly, reduce the heat slightly
