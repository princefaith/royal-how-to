How to Boil Eggs Perfectly Every Time

>

Boiling eggs seems simple, but getting them just right can be tricky. This guide will show you how to make perfect boiled eggs every time, whether you like them soft, medium, or hard boiled.

What You'll Need

Gather these simple items:

  • Fresh eggs (as many as you want to cook)
  • A medium-sized pot with lid
  • Water
  • A timer or clock
  • A bowl of ice water (for cooling)
  • A slotted spoon
Eggs and Pot Image

Step 1: Prepare the Eggs

Place the eggs in a single layer at the bottom of your pot. Don't stack them on top of each other.

If your eggs are straight from the refrigerator, let them sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes before boiling. This helps prevent cracking.

Step 1 Image

Step 2: Add Water and Heat

Add enough cold water to the pot to cover the eggs by about one inch.

Place the pot on the stove and turn the heat to high. Bring the water to a rolling boil.

Step 2 Image

Step 3: Cooking Time

Once the water is boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the eggs simmer.

Set your timer based on how you like your eggs:

  • 4-6 minutes for soft boiled (runny yolk)
  • 7-9 minutes for medium boiled (slightly firm yolk)
  • 10-12 minutes for hard boiled (fully firm yolk)
Step 3 Image

Step 4: Cool the Eggs

When the timer goes off, immediately remove the pot from heat.

Use a slotted spoon to transfer the eggs to a bowl of ice water. Let them cool for at least 5 minutes.

This stops the cooking process and makes the eggs easier to peel.

Step 4 Image

Step 5: Peel and Serve

Gently tap the cooled egg on a hard surface to crack the shell all over.

Peel under running water to help remove the shell easily.

Your perfectly boiled eggs are ready to eat or use in recipes!

Step 5 Image

Helpful Tips

  • Older eggs are easier to peel than very fresh eggs
  • Add a teaspoon of vinegar to the water to help prevent cracking
  • For easier peeling, start with eggs that are at room temperature
  • If an egg cracks while boiling, add a little vinegar to the water to help seal it
  • Hard boiled eggs can be stored in the refrigerator for up to one week
