How to Cook Perfect Rice Every Time

Cooking rice seems simple, but getting it just right can be tricky. This guide will show you how to make perfect fluffy rice that's not too sticky or too dry. Once you learn this basic method, you'll use it for years.

What You'll Need

Gather these simple items:

  • 1 cup of white rice (long grain works best)
  • 2 cups of water
  • Â½ teaspoon of salt (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of butter or oil (optional)
  • A medium-sized pot with a tight-fitting lid
  • A measuring cup
Rice & Pot Image

Step 1: Rinse the Rice

Place the rice in a fine mesh strainer. Run cold water over it while gently stirring with your fingers.

Continue rinsing until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and keeps the rice from getting too sticky.

Rinsing Rice Image

Step 2: Measure Water and Combine

For most white rice, use a 1:2 ratio - one cup of rice to two cups of water.

Put the rinsed rice in your pot. Add the water, salt, and butter or oil if you're using them.

Measuring Water Image

Step 3: Bring to a Boil

Place the pot on the stove over high heat. Bring the water to a rolling boil.

Once it's boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with the lid.

Boiling Water Image

Step 4: Simmer and Steam

Let the rice simmer for 18-20 minutes. Don't lift the lid during this time - the steam is cooking the rice.

After 18 minutes, check if all the water has been absorbed. If not, cook for another 2-3 minutes.

Simmering Rice Image

Step 5: Let it Rest

Remove the pot from heat but keep the lid on. Let the rice sit for 5-10 minutes.

This resting time allows the rice to finish cooking evenly and become fluffy.

Resting Rice Image

Step 6: Fluff and Serve

Remove the lid and fluff the rice with a fork. This separates the grains and releases extra steam.

Your rice is now ready to serve. Enjoy it with your favorite dishes.

Fluffing Rice Image

Helpful Tips

  • Brown rice needs more water (1:2.5 ratio) and longer cooking time (40-45 minutes)
  • Don't stir rice while it's cooking - this releases starch and makes it sticky
  • If rice is too wet after cooking, leave the lid off for a few minutes
  • If rice is too dry, sprinkle a tablespoon of water over it and let it steam for 5 more minutes
  • For more flavor, cook rice in broth instead of water
